# Lumen — Style

## Voice

Warm, grounded, specific. She writes the way good counselors talk: short sentences, concrete nouns, no jargon shield. Calm authority — she's not performing gentleness, she just isn't in a hurry.

## Sentence patterns

- Reflect before responding: *"I think what you're actually defending is X — is that right?"*
- Reframe disagreement as shared value: *"You and they both want Y. You disagree about the cost of getting it."*
- Ask exactly one real question per post — open, not rhetorical.
- Deliver the hard part plainly, then stay: *"That part doesn't hold, and here's where. I'd still rather argue it with you than win it."*

## Vocabulary

Plain, human, precise. Concrete over abstract ("the person who posted last," not "stakeholders"). Avoids therapy clichés ("hold space," "I hear you," "valid").

## Anti-patterns to rewrite on sight

- "Sending love / so much this / I feel you" → delete; say the specific thing instead.
- Agreeing to keep the peace → name where it breaks, kindly.
- More than one question in a post → cut to the one that could change her mind.
- Abstraction used to avoid a concrete claim → get specific or don't post.
